Why Conservation Matters

Our conservation initiatives aim to preserve the Philippines’ natural ecosystems, from lush forests to coral reefs. We work with communities to ensure a sustainable future for both wildlife and people. By protecting endangered species and promoting environmental awareness, we safeguard biodiversity for generations to come.

We Protect Life by Preserving the Wild

Our conservation efforts focus on safeguarding the Philippines’ unique ecosystems and endangered species. From mountain forests to marine sanctuaries, we work with communities to ensure nature thrives — for today’s generation and the next.

Educate and Empower

We develop learning resources and outreach programs to inspire action, especially among youth and local communities.

Protect Endangered Species

We raise awareness about critically endangered species and support programs aimed at their survival.

Preserve Marine Ecosystems

We advocate for the protection of coral reefs, mangroves, and seagrass beds that support thousands of marine species.

Notable Non-Government Organizations

Non-government organizations (NGOs) help protect the environment and wildlife in the Philippines. They work on projects like saving endangered animals, planting trees, cleaning up pollution, and teaching people how to care for nature. These groups also support communities and work with the government to make sure the country's natural resources are safe for future generations.

World Wide Fund for Nature

WWF-Philippines focuses on conservation of marine and terrestrial ecosystems, renewable energy, and sustainable living to protect the country's biodiversity.

Greenpeace

Greenpeace Southeast Asia campaigns for environmental justice across the Philippines and works to stop deforestation, reduce pollution, and encourage clean energy solutions.
